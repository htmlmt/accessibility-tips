Here are the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines: https://www.w3.org/WAI/standards-guidelines/wcag/glance/ These rules are what define whether a site is accessible, based on three levels of conformance: A, AA, and AAA Deque is a company that maintains the axe browser extensions, which can check for some accessibility issues: https://www.deque.com/axe/browser-extensions/ It's also a good resource for accessibility news and tips: https://www.deque.com/accessible-development/ DevTools has a number of features that can ease the development of accessible code: In particular, this is how you would emulate how a site might be seen by someone with color blindness: https://developer.chrome.com/blog/new-in-devtools-83/#vision-deficiencies
